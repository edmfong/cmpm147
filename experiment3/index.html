Experiment 3 - Alternate Worlds



Description

This experiemnt displays procedural generation utilizing autotiling.
The theme of the overworld is a lone house in the woods next to a lake as the seasons change.
The theme of the dungeon is a typical RPG dungeon featuring randomly generated loot or mobs in each room.

Technical

Overworld:
The world is procedurally generated utilizing noise to randomize the water, and land.
To make the world more random, the trees and lilypads are randomly placed based on noise.
Based on the theme, only one duck and house are randomly placed.

Dungeon:
The dungeon is procedurally generated utilizing noise and the dungeon size is randomized.
To make each dungeon room more random, the loot and mobs are randomly placed.

Reflection

Overall, the experiment was harder than expected. There were some hiccups along the way, such as implementing the autotiling and messing with noise variables. One of the biggest hurdles I faced was generating and connecting hallways in the dungeons. One thing that went successfully was that changing the tiles based on the time worked well. * ChatGPT was a major help throughout this project.
